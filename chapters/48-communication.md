## 48.1 Technical Communication with @tech-communicator

### Skill Introduction

The `@tech-communicator` skill provides expertise in explaining technical concepts clearly. It helps you adapt your communication to different audiences and convey complex information effectively.

**When to use this skill:**
- Explaining technical decisions to stakeholders
- Writing technical documentation
- Presenting architecture proposals
- Status reporting

**Key strengths:**
- Audience adaptation
- Complex concept simplification
- Visual communication

---

### Technical Communication Prompts

#### Presenting Technical Decision to Stakeholders

**Context:** You need to explain a major technical decision to non-technical stakeholders.

```text
@tech-communicator Help me communicate a technical decision to executives:

Technical decision:
- Migrating from monolith to microservices
- 12-month project, $500K investment
- Some features delayed during migration
- Long-term benefits: scalability, velocity

Audience:
- CEO (business focused, limited tech background)
- CFO (wants ROI and cost justification)
- VP Product (concerned about feature delays)

Their likely concerns:
- Why is this necessary now?
- What's the risk of not doing it?
- How does this affect our product roadmap?
- What happens if it fails?

Please provide:
1. Executive summary (1 paragraph)
2. Business case framing
3. Visual aids suggestions
4. Analogy for microservices concept
5. Risk communication approach
6. ROI narrative
7. Roadmap impact presentation
8. FAQ preparation
```

**Expected Output:** Executive-ready presentation materials.

---

#### Writing Technical Proposals

## Best Practices Summary

### Communication
1. **Know Your Audience:** Adapt style and content
2. **Be Concise:** Respect people's time
3. **Visual Aids:** Use diagrams and charts
4. **Listen First:** Understand before responding
5. **Follow Up:** Confirm understanding

### Meetings
1. **Purpose First:** Clear meeting goals
2. **Right People:** Only necessary attendees
3. **Time-Box:** Stick to allocated time
4. **Decisions:** Document outcomes
5. **Async Default:** Meet only when needed

### Remote
1. **Over-Communicate:** More context in writing
2. **Document Everything:** Accessible knowledge
3. **Intentional Bonding:** Schedule social time
4. **Timezone Respect:** Fair meeting distribution
5. **Video Optional:** Reduce fatigue
