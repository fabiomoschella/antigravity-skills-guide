## Overview

Career development for software engineers extends beyond technical skills. Growing your career requires intentional planning, continuous learning, and developing both technical depth and leadership capabilities.

This chapter covers essential skills for professional growth, from individual contributor paths to leadership transitions.

## 50.1 Career Planning with @career-coach

### Skill Introduction

The `@career-coach` skill provides expertise in engineering career development. It helps you plan your career path, identify growth areas, and make strategic career decisions.

**When to use this skill:**
- Career path planning
- Promotion preparation
- Job change decisions
- Skill gap identification

**Key strengths:**
- Engineering career ladders
- Growth planning
- Decision frameworks

---

### Career Planning Prompts

#### Mapping Career Path

**Context:** You're unsure about your next career steps.

```text
@career-coach Help me plan my engineering career path:

Current situation:
- Senior Software Engineer (5 years experience)
- Strong in backend development (Python, Go)
- Comfortable with system design
- At current company 3 years

Interests and strengths:
- Enjoy solving complex technical problems
- Comfortable mentoring juniors
- Not sure about people management appeal
- Interested in distributed systems

Uncertainties:
- Staff Engineer vs Engineering Manager?
- Stay generalist or specialize?
- When to change companies?
- How to build influence?

Goals:
- Increase impact and compensation
- Work on challenging problems
- Maintain technical depth

Please provide:
1. Career path options analysis
2. IC vs Management comparison
3. Skills to develop for each path
4. Specialization considerations
5. Visibility and influence building
6. Compensation growth strategies
7. Timeline expectations
8. Decision framework
```

**Expected Output:** Personalized career development plan.

### Tech Leadership Prompts

#### Transitioning to Tech Lead

**Context:** You're moving from senior engineer to tech lead.

```text
@tech-lead-coach Help me transition to tech lead role:

Situation:
- Just promoted to tech lead
- Team of 6 engineers
- Responsible for delivery and technical direction
- Still expected to contribute code

Concerns:
- Less coding time than before
- Unsure how to distribute my time
- Never managed people formally
- Worried about losing technical edge
- Team members with more domain expertise

Team dynamics:
- Mix of junior and senior engineers
- Some skepticism about my promotion
- Previous lead was very hands-on

Please provide:
1. First 90 days plan
2. Time allocation framework
3. Earning team's trust
4. Technical vs leadership balance
5. Delegation strategies
6. Running effective 1:1s
7. Technical decision process
8. Common pitfalls to avoid
```

**Expected Output:** Tech lead transition guide.

## Best Practices Summary

### Career Growth
1. **Be Intentional:** Plan your development
2. **Seek Feedback:** Regular input
3. **Stretch Projects:** Take on challenges
4. **Build Network:** Relationships matter
5. **Document Impact:** Track achievements

### Leadership
1. **Serve the Team:** Enable others
2. **Earn Trust:** Through actions
3. **Delegate:** Develop others
4. **Communicate:** Overcommunicate
5. **Stay Technical:** Maintain credibility

### Interviews
1. **Practice:** Consistent preparation
2. **Mock Interviews:** Simulate real conditions
3. **Tell Stories:** Behavioral prep
4. **Ask Questions:** Show interest
5. **Negotiate:** Know your worth
